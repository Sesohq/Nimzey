To get the slider back to its normal “drag-and-slide” behavior and restrict node dragging to the header only, you need to stop React Flow’s drag handler from kicking in everywhere except where you actually want it (i.e. the header), and also make sure pointer events on your <input type="range"> don’t bubble up to React Flow either.

What changed?
Wrap the entire body of the node (everything below the header) in a <div onPointerDown={e => e.stopPropagation()}>.
This prevents pointer down events from ever reaching React Flow’s node‐drag handler, so clicking or dragging anywhere in the body (including on your sliders, selects, labels, etc.) won’t drag the node.

Keep the header alone, so pointer down on the header still bubbles up to React Flow and starts the drag. The cursor-move on the header gives a visual affordance that that’s where you drag from.

Add onPointerDown/onPointerUp (in addition to your existing onMouseDown/onTouchStart) on the <input type="range">.
Modern browsers dispatch pointer events when dragging the thumb, so you need to stop those too.

With these two tweaks:

Your slider regains normal drag‐and‐slide behavior.

The node moves only when you grab its header bar.
